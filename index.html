What We Offer

Three things we help with — all explained in plain English.

Final Expense Insurance

Affordable coverage that protects your family from funeral costs and end-of-life expenses. Typically $5,000–$25,000. Most plans require no medical exam.

  • Covers funeral & burial costs
  • Premiums stay level — never go up
  • Available even with health conditions
  • Coverage never expires while you pay

Medicare Guidance

We explain how Medicare Parts A, B, C, and D actually work — and help you choose between Supplement and Advantage plans with confidence. Education first, always.

  • Turning 65 & enrolling for first time
  • Reviewing your current coverage
  • Understanding what your plan covers
  • Open enrollment guidance

Free Policy Reviews

Already have coverage? We'll review it at no charge and tell you honestly whether you have the right plan at the right price — or if there's something better available.

  • Are your premiums reasonable?
  • Are there gaps you don't know about?
  • Could you get the same for less?
  • No obligation to make any changes

Common Questions

It's okay to be skeptical. Here are honest answers.

"Is this a scam?"

We understand why you'd ask. Illinois Senior Benefit Services has been serving Illinois seniors since 2008. We're licensed and local — you can verify our license through the Illinois Department of Insurance. We're not a telemarketer. We're your neighbors.

"Do I have to buy something?"

Absolutely not. Many people call us just to understand their options. You'll never feel pressured. If you decide to do nothing, that's completely fine. Our goal is for you to feel informed.

"How much does this cost me?"

Our guidance is free to you. Like most insurance agents, we're paid by the insurance companies if you enroll in a plan. That never changes what we recommend — we only show plans that genuinely fit your needs.

"What if I already have coverage?"

We'll review it for free. Sometimes existing coverage is just right. Sometimes we find gaps, overcharges, or better alternatives. Either way, you'll know where you stand — and there's no obligation to change anything.
